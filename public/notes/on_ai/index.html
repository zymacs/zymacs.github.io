Thoughts on Control in the Age of AI

I have a lot of personal opinion on AI. Most of it shaped around my experience using LLMS. I think AI is not LLMs. I think the conflation of the term AI and LLMs is non trivial. But that's just one of the points about which I'd like to keep notes in this document. Other areas include how thoughts on if we need LLMs, how to outsource to humble tools what is said to be only accomplishable with LLMs. How to stay as in control as someone using a CLI when using tools that are LLM like, opinions on why LLMs are sold as they are and anything I find of interest in relation to this topic.

Definitions

  • What is AI
  • What is an LLM

LLMs are AI. AI is not LLMs. On the importance of the difference

Sure if you ask most today about AI, the first thought that'll come to mind is ChatGPT. And that ain't just non-technical folk for whom it would be understandable. They most likely only got to interface with AI on the advent of LLMs. This applies to a lot of technical people too. I conflate the terms very often as well personally.
